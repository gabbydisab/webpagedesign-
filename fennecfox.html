Fennec Fox - Vulpes Zerda

Basic Information on the Fennec Fox:

The fennec fox is the smallest of all the world's foxes!! These foxes live in the sandy Sahara and in North Africa. Their nocturnal habits that help them survive the heat of the desert environment, and some physical adaptations help as well.

Why is the Fennec Fox endangered?

As a native to the Sahara desert and other areas in North Africa, the fennec fox uses its ears and other adaptations that allow them to deal with the intense heat. Their ears help to dissipate heat and locate small prey. Their fur is also thick to keep them safe from the sun and to insulate their bodies when the temperature drops drastically during the night.

How much does a Fennec Fox cost?

This endangered species costs approximately 1,500-2,000 dollars! Most people usually get placed onto a waiting list for a year or more to obtain a kit from a breeder. Generally these animals have trouble getting used to a new home because they tend to bond with their first owner.
